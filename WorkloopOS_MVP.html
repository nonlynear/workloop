CHRO Workforce Navigation Framework
For every employee affected by AI role redesign, the platform generates a Decision Pathway — a structured framework that guides HR through the right action for each person.
Path A: Upskill in Role
62%
Role is redesigned but the person stays. Skills gap is bridgeable (< 40 training hours). AI augments their work — they become more valuable.
HR Action: Skills gap map → LMS enrollment → updated JD → performance review against new competencies
Path B: Internal Redeployment
23%
Current role significantly displaced, but transferable skills match open or growing roles. Person moves to a new position internally.
HR Action: Skills matching → internal opportunity mapping → transition plan → 90-day onboarding in new role
Path C: Transition Support
10%
Role consolidation or significant displacement. Skill transition attempted but timeline is 6+ months. Enhanced support package while transitioning.
HR Action: Extended training program → mentorship pairing → interim role assignment → quarterly progress review
Path D: Managed Separation
5%
Role eliminated or person unable to complete transition. All internal options exhausted. Legally compliant, humane separation with support.
HR Action: Consultation period (jurisdiction-specific) → fair selection criteria → severance package → outplacement support → alumni network
Why this matters legally: Under the EU AI Act (Aug 2026), companies must demonstrate that AI-driven workforce decisions include proper human oversight, non-discrimination, and documented processes. This Decision Pathway framework — with its audit trail of assessments, training offered, and alternatives explored — is the compliance evidence regulators will look for. The CLO co-founder's employment law expertise across 6 APAC jurisdictions ensures separation processes meet local legal requirements in every country.
